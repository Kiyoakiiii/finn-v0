# Finn Wellness Knowledge Base

This small knowledge base is intentionally simple and curated for a v0 prototype. In production, content should be reviewed, versioned, and updated by qualified owners.

## Sleep Basics

Healthy sleep habits usually start with consistency. Going to bed and waking up around the same time helps the body build a stable rhythm. A short wind-down routine, dimmer light, less caffeine late in the day, and keeping the bedroom cool and quiet can support better sleep quality.

## Stress Management

Stress management works best when it is small and repeatable. Short breathing exercises, brief walks, journaling, stretching, and taking breaks from screens can help the nervous system settle. A user who feels overwhelmed may benefit from choosing one manageable action instead of trying to change everything at once.

## Hydration

Hydration needs vary based on body size, activity, weather, diet, and health status. A practical habit is to drink water after waking, with meals, and after exercise. Thirst, urine color, and energy can be useful signals, but persistent symptoms should be discussed with a healthcare professional.

## Movement and Activity

Regular movement supports energy, mood, sleep, and general health. For someone starting from a low activity baseline, a short walk, gentle mobility routine, or light bodyweight session can be a better starting point than an intense workout. The best activity plan is one the user can repeat.

## Nutrition Basics

Helpful nutrition habits often focus on simple structure rather than strict rules. A balanced meal can include protein, fiber-rich carbohydrates, healthy fats, and fruits or vegetables. Users should avoid extreme restriction and seek professional guidance for medical nutrition needs or eating disorder concerns.

## Habit Building

Habits are easier to build when they are specific, small, and attached to an existing routine. A useful v0 coaching pattern is to ask what action can be completed in under two minutes, when it will happen, and what cue will trigger it. Progress tracking should feel supportive, not punitive.

## Mindfulness

Mindfulness is the practice of noticing the present moment without immediately judging it. Short practices such as three slow breaths, a body scan, or naming five things the user can see may help create a pause during stress. Mindfulness is a skill that improves with repetition.
